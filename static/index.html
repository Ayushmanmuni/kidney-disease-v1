Understanding the Disease

What is Chronic Kidney Disease?

CKD is a progressive condition where kidneys gradually lose their ability to filter blood properly

🫘

About CKD

Chronic Kidney Disease is the gradual loss of kidney function over months or years. Kidneys filter waste and excess fluids from blood. When damaged, waste builds up to dangerous levels in the body.

🌍

Global Impact

850 million people worldwide are affected. CKD causes 2.4 million deaths/year, making it the 6th fastest growing cause of death globally. 1 in 10 adults has some form of CKD.

⏰

Early Detection Matters

90% of CKD patients are unaware of their condition. Early-stage CKD is treatable and manageable. AI-powered screening can catch it before symptoms appear, potentially saving lives.

Clinical Biomarkers

Key Parameters for CKD Detection

The most important clinical indicators used by our model

🧪

Serum Creatinine

Waste product from muscle metabolism filtered by kidneys. Elevated levels (>1.2 mg/dL) are one of the strongest indicators of impaired kidney function.

Normal: 0.6–1.2 mg/dL
🩸

Hemoglobin

Kidneys produce erythropoietin to stimulate red blood cell production. Low hemoglobin (<12 g/dL) indicates anemia, a common CKD complication.

Normal: 12–17 g/dL
💉

Blood Pressure

High blood pressure damages the small blood vessels in kidneys over time. It is both a leading cause and a consequence of kidney disease.

Normal: 60–80 mm/Hg
🍬

Blood Glucose

Diabetes is the #1 cause of CKD globally. Persistently high blood sugar damages the kidney's filtering units (glomeruli).

Normal: 70–140 mg/dL
💧

Blood Urea

Urea is a byproduct of protein metabolism. When kidneys can't filter effectively, blood urea nitrogen rises above normal levels.

Normal: 7–20 mg/dL
Disease Progression

The 5 Stages of CKD

CKD progresses through stages based on kidney filtration rate (GFR)

1

Mild Damage

GFR ≥ 90 · Kidney damage with normal function

2

Early Decline

GFR 60–89 · Mild loss of function

3

Moderate Damage

GFR 30–59 · Noticeable loss of function

4

Severe Damage

GFR 15–29 · Severe loss of function

5

Kidney Failure

GFR < 15 · Dialysis or transplant needed
